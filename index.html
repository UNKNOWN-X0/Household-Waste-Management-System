What this system does

♺

Stops the mix-back problem

High-value streams (paper, recyclables, organic) never enter the municipal truck. They go to saleng, Bokashi, or drop-off points — so collectors physically cannot reverse your sorting.

☯

Turns waste into income

Sorted aluminium, PET, paper, and cardboard are assets. A regular saleng relationship earns 50–200 ฿/month and creates a financial incentive stronger than any penalty system.

◈

Works in Thailand's climate

Bokashi fermentation replaces German brown-bin composting — it handles oily Thai food, seals against fruit flies, and produces liquid fertiliser. No outdoor pile needed.

Organic waste: the Thai challenge

Most Thai municipalities don't collect organic waste separately yet. You process it at home — and Thailand's heat actually accelerates breakdown faster than Germany.

Recommended for Thai homes

Bokashi composting · โบกาฉิ

Anaerobic fermentation in a sealed bucket with inoculated bran. Unlike a compost pile, Bokashi handles all food waste including cooked food and small amounts of meat and fish — the dominant waste types in a Thai kitchen.

1Layer food scraps in sealed bucket, sprinkle Bokashi bran, press to remove air, seal lid.
2Drain liquid every few days. Dilute 1:100 with water → excellent fertiliser for plants.
3When full, let ferment 2 weeks. Bury in soil or large pot to finish composting.
4Rotate two buckets so one is always available. No smell, no insects, no rodents.

Can't compost at home?

Freeze & donate

Freeze scraps to kill pests, then donate to a local temple, school, or community garden that composts. Many Bangkok temples run composting programs.

Municipal green bin

Check with your เทศบาล — many Bangkok districts and some provincial cities now collect organic waste separately on designated days.

Trash Hero Thailand

Community-based cleanup and composting network. Good for bulk organic donation and connecting with local eco-communities.

The mix-back problem — and the fix

The core challenge: collectors dump everything into one truck. The solution is to remove your valuable streams from municipal collection entirely, so they can never be mixed back.

Golden rule Only the blue residual bag ever goes into the public truck. All other streams have dedicated, separate destinations.

Outbound routes by stream

Organic → Bokashi bucket or home compost Zero municipal contact
Paper → Saleng / ธนาคารขยะ Sells for 3–8 ฿/kg
Recyclables → Saleng / GEPP / WONGPANit Aluminium 30–50 ฿/kg
Residual → Municipal truck (blue bag only) True non-recyclables only

If you must use the municipal truck for all bags

Colour-code everything

Use clearly different coloured bags for each stream. Translucent yellow bags show contents visibly. Attach waterproof Thai-language tags to each bag identifying the stream.

Put bags out last-minute

Only put bags out 15–30 minutes before the truck arrives. Bags left overnight get mixed by informal collectors or rain damage destroys the colour coding.

Talk to your collector

Introduce yourself, explain you're separating waste, ask them to keep bags separate. Offer cold water or snacks. Personal rapport changes behaviour more than any label.

Labelled outdoor bins

Place official-coloured bins outside with matching bags inside. Visual separation of bins makes it harder for collectors to mix casually.

Informal waste buyer with truck or tricycle. Set a regular Saturday pickup. Zero chance of stream mixing.

Onboarding checklist

4-week implementation roadmap

Start simple: organic + recyclables + residual is enough in week one. Add paper separation once the household is comfortable.

Week 1
Setup & purchase
  • Buy 5 bins + coloured bags
  • Print and laminate sorting poster
  • Call เทศบาล re: collection days
  • Find nearest saleng / waste bank
  • Find hazardous drop-off point
  • Order Bokashi bucket + bran
  • Position all bins in place
Week 2
Soft launch
  • Family meeting + walkthrough
  • Practice sort a bag of mixed trash
  • Warning-only week — no fines yet
  • Start Bokashi first bucket
  • Begin bundling paper separately
  • Train any household helpers
  • Put up Thai labels on all bins
Week 3
Go live
  • Fines begin (20 ฿/violation)
  • Contact saleng for first pickup
  • Evening bin checks begin
  • First blue bag out on collection day
  • First Bokashi bucket full → bury
  • Hazardous box: begin filling
  • Jar on counter, visible to all
Week 4
Review & optimise
  • Measure residual bag volume
  • Adjust bin sizes if needed
  • Count saleng income earned
  • First hazardous drop-off visit
  • Update poster for any confusion
  • Celebrate first reward if earned
  • Share system with one neighbour

Ongoing maintenance

Weekly
  • Wash organic bin with soap + water
  • Rinse recycling bin (prevents ants)
  • Check Bokashi drain spigot, empty liquid
  • Sanitise residual bin lid
  • Rotate Sorting Inspector role
Monthly
  • Estimate residual bag volume — aim to reduce each month
  • Log saleng / app income in household journal
  • Bokashi compost → garden or potted plants
  • Hazardous drop-off at Big C / HomePro / อบต.
  • Review reward chart, pay out stars
Pest tips — Thai climate
  • Fruit flies: tight lid + empty organic daily + Bokashi bran layer on top
  • Ants: wipe bin rims with diluted vinegar or peppermint oil
  • Cockroaches: sealed bins, clean immediately after cooking
  • Rodents: Bokashi is fully sealed — no rodent access

Printable Thai labels

Print, laminate, and attach above each bin. Bilingual — Thai household members and helpers can both read them.
