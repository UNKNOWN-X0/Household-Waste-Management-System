Why organic needs its own strategy in Thailand

Most Thai municipalities don't separately collect organic waste yet. You must process it at home — the tropical heat actually makes this faster than in Germany.
Bokashi (recommended)
Anaerobic fermentation in a sealed bucket with inoculated bran (โบกาฉิ). Handles all food including cooked food and small amounts of meat/fish — a major advantage over regular compost. Two buckets rotate. Drain liquid every few days (dilute 1:100 as fertiliser). After 2 weeks, bury pre-compost in soil or a large pot. No flies, no smell (slightly pickled). Buy bran on Lazada: "โบกาฉิ".
Compost pile / bin
Best with a garden. Layer green (food scraps) with brown (dried leaves, shredded paper, coconut coir). Thailand's heat means fast breakdown — turn weekly. Cover to deter rats. Not suitable for meat/fish without hot composting.
Vermicomposting
Worm farm (ไส้เดือนดิน) using Eisenia fetida. Excellent fertiliser output. Requires shade and moisture control. Avoid excess citrus, onion, spicy food. Good for condo balconies.
Can't compost?
Freeze scraps to kill pests, then donate to a local temple, school, or community garden that composts. Or check if your เทศบาล has started organic collection — many Bangkok districts now do.

The core problem: collectors mixing streams back

The solution is to remove high-value streams (paper + recyclables) from municipal collection entirely. Only the blue residual bag enters the public truck.
Sell to ซาเล้ง (best option)
Build a relationship with a local saleng or "รับซื้อของเก่า" shop. Arrange a regular Saturday pickup. They pay by weight and sort further themselves — zero chance of mixing. Typical prices: paper 3–5 ฿/kg, cardboard 5–8 ฿/kg, aluminium cans 30–50 ฿/kg. A family earns 50–200 ฿/month — use it as a household treat fund.
ธนาคารขยะ (waste bank)
Many temples, schools, and villages run waste banks. Bring pre-sorted recyclables, get credited as savings. Guarantees correct processing. Search "ธนาคารขยะ ใกล้ฉัน".
Apps: GEPP / WONGPANit
GEPP (เก็บ) schedules recyclable pickup from your home. WONGPANit (วงษ์พานิช) is a major scrap chain with transparent per-kg pricing. Both available in Bangkok and major cities.
If using municipal truck
Only the blue residual bag goes out. Use clearly different coloured bags. Attach waterproof Thai tags (see Labels tab). Put out bags 15–30 min before the truck — not overnight. Place recyclables in a separate labelled outdoor bin so they're visually distinct.
Coordinate with neighbors
One sorting household is easy to ignore. Ten households on the same street force collectors to adapt. Share the poster and bag colours — propose a shared saleng pickup day for the street.
Self-haul (maximum control)
Deliver sorted recyclables and hazardous waste yourself to a municipal recycling centre or "ห้างรับซื้อของเก่า". Total control, often better prices. Worth doing for hazardous items monthly.
